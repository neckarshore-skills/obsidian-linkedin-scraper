## GDPR (EU users)

If you process the personal data of any natural person located in the EU/EEA/UK, **you act as the data controller**. Article 14 GDPR requires you to inform that person within one month of obtaining their data. The "disproportionate effort" exemption (Art. 14(5)(b)) has been narrowly construed by EU regulators (Polish DPA, *Bisnode*, 2019, EUR 220,000 fine, appeals exhausted 2022). LinkedIn data is frequently directly identifying (full name, employer, role) — assume Article 14 applies.
